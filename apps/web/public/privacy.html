Central Command

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 3 June 2026

Central Command is a personal performance dashboard that brings a single user's calendar, weather, fitness, nutrition, sleep, gaming, and news data together in one interface. This policy explains what data the application accesses through Google APIs, how that data is used and stored, and the choices you have. It applies to the application hosted at centralcommand.studiosc.dev.

Operator & contact

Central Command is operated as a personal project. For any question about this policy or your data, contact admin@studiosc.dev.

Google data we access

When you choose to connect your Google account, we request these scopes:

  • openid, email, profile — to identify your account and associate your data with you.
  • Google Calendar (read-only, calendar.readonly) — to read your upcoming events so the dashboard can display them and compute a duration-based "busyness" score for your day.

We request the minimum scopes needed for these features. We do not request write access to your calendar, and we never modify, create, or delete your events.

How we use the data

  • Display your upcoming calendar events on your private dashboard.
  • Calculate a daily busyness score from the duration of your events.

Your Google data is used only to provide these features to you. It is never used for advertising, never sold, and never used to train any machine learning or AI model.

How we store and protect the data

  • OAuth access and refresh tokens are encrypted at rest before being stored in our database (Cloudflare D1).
  • Calendar events are cached transiently (about five minutes) in Cloudflare KV to reduce API calls, then expire automatically.
  • All traffic is served over HTTPS, and the dashboard sits behind zero-trust access controls (Cloudflare Access).

Sharing and disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or share your Google data with third parties. Data is processed only on Cloudflare's infrastructure, which provides the hosting, database, and caching for this application. We may disclose data if required by law.

Limited Use disclosure

Central Command's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Retention and deletion

  • You can revoke Central Command's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page. Revoking access stops all further data access immediately.
  • To have your stored tokens and associated data deleted, disconnect the integration or email admin@studiosc.dev. Cached calendar data expires on its own within minutes.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected here with an updated "last updated" date.
